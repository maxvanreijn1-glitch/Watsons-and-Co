How It Works

Your Guide to
Renting a Property

01

Register & Tell Us What You Need

Contact our lettings team to discuss your requirements in detail. We want to understand not just the basics — location, size, budget — but the lifestyle you are looking for, so we can match you with the right property as quickly as possible. We will alert you to suitable properties immediately as they become available, including off-market opportunities.

02

Viewings

We arrange and accompany all viewings and will ensure you have the time and information needed to make a fully considered decision. Our team can typically arrange viewings within 24 to 48 hours of your enquiry. Evening and weekend appointments are available on request.

03

Holding Deposit

When you decide to proceed with a property, we will take a holding deposit equivalent to one week's rent (in accordance with the Tenant Fees Act 2019). This reserves the property while referencing is completed. The holding deposit is deducted from your first month's rent on move-in, provided referencing is satisfactory.

04

Referencing

We conduct thorough referencing through an independent agency. This typically includes credit checks, employment and income verification, and references from your previous landlord. We aim to complete referencing within three to five working days. For self-employed applicants or those with complex income, we will advise on the documentation required.

05

Tenancy Agreement & Deposit

Once referencing is complete, we will prepare your tenancy agreement and arrange for all parties to sign. Prior to move-in, you will pay your security deposit (a maximum of five weeks' rent for annual rents under £50,000). Your deposit is protected throughout your tenancy in a government-approved deposit protection scheme.

06

Move In & Ongoing Support

On move-in day, we conduct a thorough check-in inspection and provide you with a detailed inventory of the property's condition. For fully managed properties, our team remains your point of contact throughout your tenancy for any maintenance or management matters. We aim to respond to all maintenance issues within 24 hours.

In accordance with the Tenant Fees Act 2019, the only payments we may request from tenants are:

As agreed in the tenancy agreement

Maximum five weeks' rent (six weeks if annual rent is £50,000+)

Where We Let

Our Rental Areas

Jesmond

Consistently Newcastle's most popular rental location, Jesmond attracts a wide range of tenants including professionals, academics, and families. The area offers excellent transport links via the Metro, a wonderful selection of restaurants, cafés and independent shops, and Jesmond Dene park on the doorstep. Rental properties range from well-presented studio and one-bedroom apartments to spacious family homes.

Typical rents: £1,200 – £3,000 pcm

Quayside

Newcastle's iconic Quayside is the city's most vibrant district, home to award-winning restaurants, bars, galleries and cultural venues alongside some of the most striking riverside architecture in the north of England. Rental properties here are predominantly high-specification apartments with spectacular views across the Tyne, attracting city-minded professionals seeking a truly urban lifestyle.

Typical rents: £1,500 – £4,000 pcm

Gosforth

A favourite with families, Gosforth offers excellent schools, a charming high street, and a welcoming community atmosphere. The area's varied rental stock includes Victorian and Edwardian terraces, semi-detached homes, and purpose-built apartments. Gosforth is exceptionally well connected to Newcastle city centre by road and public transport, making it a practical as well as a pleasant place to live.

Typical rents: £1,100 – £2,500 pcm

Sandyford & Heaton

Sandyford and Heaton offer an excellent range of characterful period properties at attractive rents, positioned just minutes from Jesmond and the city centre. Both areas are particularly popular with young professionals and academics affiliated with Newcastle University and Northumbria University. The neighbourhoods are lively, diverse, and well-served by local amenities and public transport.

Typical rents: £900 – £1,800 pcm
