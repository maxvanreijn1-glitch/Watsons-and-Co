Anti-Money Laundering Policy

Last updated: May 2026  ·  Version 1.0

Legal Requirement Watsons & Co is supervised by HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) for the purposes of the Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds (Information on the Payer) Regulations 2017 (MLRs 2017). Our HMRC AML registration number is XMWAT00000123456. Compliance with these regulations is a legal requirement, and we are unable to proceed with any transaction without satisfactory completion of our identity and verification checks.

1. Our Legal Obligations

As an estate agency business, we are a "relevant person" for the purposes of the MLRs 2017. This means we are legally required to:

  • Identify and verify the identity of all clients (buyers, sellers, landlords and tenants) before entering into a business relationship or carrying out an occasional transaction
  • Understand the nature and purpose of the business relationship
  • Conduct ongoing monitoring of business relationships
  • Apply enhanced due diligence (EDD) in higher-risk situations
  • Report suspicious activity to the National Crime Agency (NCA) where required
  • Keep records of all identification and verification documents for a minimum of 5 years

2. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

Before we can act for you, we must carry out Customer Due Diligence. This involves:

Identity Verification

We will ask you to provide one or more of the following documents to prove your identity and residential address:

Proof of Identity (one required)Proof of Address (one required)
Current valid passportUtility bill (gas, electric, water) dated within 3 months
Current valid UK driving licence (full or provisional)Council tax bill for the current year
National identity card (EEA/Swiss nationals)Bank or building society statement dated within 3 months
UK biometric residence permitHMRC correspondence dated within the last 12 months

Where you are acting as a company, trust or other legal entity, we will require documentation to verify the entity itself and the beneficial owners who ultimately own or control it (typically those with more than 25% ownership).

Electronic Verification

Where permitted and with your consent, we may use an electronic identity verification service to cross-reference the information you provide against credit reference agency and other databases. This process does not affect your credit score.

3. Source of Funds

We may ask you to provide evidence of the source of funds being used in a transaction. This is a standard legal requirement and applies to all clients equally, regardless of their personal circumstances. We may ask for:

  • Bank statements showing the accumulation or transfer of funds
  • Evidence of the sale of another property
  • Mortgage or financial institution documentation
  • Evidence of inheritance or gift (with donor's own identification where applicable)
  • Evidence of business income, dividends or investments

4. Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and Sanctions

We are required to apply enhanced due diligence where we identify, or have reason to believe, that a client is a Politically Exposed Person (PEP) — that is, an individual who holds, or has held, a prominent public position — or is a close family member or associate of a PEP.

We also screen all clients against HM Treasury's financial sanctions list and other relevant sanctions registers. We are unable to act for any individual or entity subject to financial sanctions.

5. Suspicious Activity

Where we have knowledge or reasonable suspicion that a transaction involves the proceeds of crime or is connected to terrorist financing, we are legally required to file a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) with the National Crime Agency. In such cases, we may be prohibited from tipping off the individual concerned about the report or from proceeding with the transaction pending consent from the NCA.

6. Consequences of Non-Compliance

If you are unable or unwilling to provide satisfactory identification and verification documents, we will be unable to act for you or to proceed with any transaction on your behalf. This applies without exception and is not a matter of discretion on our part — it is a legal requirement.

7. Record Keeping

All identification documents and records obtained as part of our AML compliance procedures will be retained for a minimum of 5 years from the end of the business relationship or the completion of the relevant transaction, whichever is later. This data is processed in accordance with our Privacy Policy.

8. Further Information

If you have any questions about our AML procedures, please contact us at hello@watsonsandco.com or +44 (0) 191 946 0123. Further guidance on money laundering regulations for estate agents is available from HMRC at gov.uk/guidance/money-laundering-regulations-estate-agency-businesses.
